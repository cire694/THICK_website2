Some writing tips

  1. Tell a story

    Humans are wired to tell stories.
    Try to tell a narrative story. That's how the admissions officers will remember you; not your name, your grade, or your classes, but your story.

  2. Use cultural language

    This effectively highlights your cultural background and can enhance your story. We'll use this story as a case study. Don't be afraid to show your background! A lot of people might balk at the idea in fear colleges might reject based on their culture, especially Asian Americans.

  3. Use personal nicknames

    Nicknames help bring the reader into your perspective. Even something as simple as replacing "My father" with "Dad" can go a long way. You can use cultural nicknames, e.g. "Grandpa" => "wai gong"

  4. 70-30 breakdown

    I recommend the following breakdown: 70% for setup/story telling, and 30% for reflection/monolouge. The reflection doesn't necessecarily have to be about growth. Again, don't force it. Just talk about how this experience changed you.

  5. Make uses of metaphors

    "I felt adrift in a sea of ambiguous identity". Stylistic choices like these are what makes essays memorable

  6. Make use of parallel structure

    From the essay linked above: "I wanted to demolish that language barrier, to jump up and down on its remains, to destory what marked me as an imposter, a pretender, a fake

    Notice that this sentence isn't gramatically correct, but it doesn't matter, it still effectively conveys the author's feelings.

  7. Know when to be descriptive

    "Pulicking gogosi", "Watching Buni spoon...". Include details that bring reader into the moment. You don't have to describe every little detail.

  8. There's no one size fits all

    Everything here are recommendations, not rules.
